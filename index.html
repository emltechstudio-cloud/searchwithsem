How it works

Three taps. That's it.

No accounts. No waiting. No paywalls. Just type, read, and explore.

01

Type or speak your search

Type a topic or tap the mic. SEM searches Wikipedia's entire knowledge base instantly.

02

Get summary and images

A clean summary with full-screen images you can swipe through. One topic, the full picture.

03

Explore related topics

Related article pills appear below every summary. Tap one to keep going without missing a beat.

04

Jump to Google or YouTube

Want more? Tap Google or YouTube — your search query carries over automatically.

Features

Built for curious minds.

Everything you need to learn anything, on any device, even without internet.

Instant Wikipedia summaries

Clean, readable summaries from Wikipedia's REST API — no clutter, no ads, no noise.

Full-screen image gallery

All article images in a tap-to-expand gallery. Swipe through them one by one, full screen.

Live trending topics

Wikipedia's most-read articles today, right on the home screen. See what the world is reading.

Related article exploration

Every summary shows related topics as clickable pills. Dive deeper without leaving the app.

Full offline support

Searched something before? It's cached. Read it again with no connection — great for low-data situations.

Voice search

Tap the mic and speak your query. SEM transcribes and searches immediately. Hands-free.

Light & dark mode

Switch between a clean light theme and a comfortable dark theme. It remembers your choice.

Installable PWA

Add to your home screen on Android or iOS. Opens like a native app. No app store needed.
